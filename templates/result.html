Common First Aid Situations and Home Treatments

Common Situation Home Treatment
Cuts and Scrapes Wash the wound with soap and water, apply an antibiotic ointment, and cover with a sterile bandage.
Burns Hold the affected area under cool running water for at least 10 minutes, then cover with a sterile, non-adhesive dressing.
Sprains and Strains Rest the injured area, apply ice for 15-20 minutes every few hours, and use compression and elevation to reduce swelling.
Nosebleeds Sit upright and lean forward, pinch your nose just below the bony part, and maintain pressure for 10-15 minutes.
Insect Bites Wash the area with soap and water, apply a cold pack to reduce swelling, and use over-the-counter anti-itch cream.
Choking Perform the Heimlich maneuver: stand behind the person and give quick upward thrusts below the ribcage.
Sunburn Take frequent cool baths or showers, apply soothing lotions that contain aloe vera, and drink plenty of water.
Stings Remove the stinger if visible, wash the area with soap and water, apply a cold pack, and use over-the-counter pain relievers.
Allergic Reactions Administer an over-the-counter antihistamine, apply a cool compress, and seek medical attention if symptoms persist.
Minor Eye Injuries Rinse the eye with clean water for 10-15 minutes, avoid rubbing the eye, and seek medical help if the irritation persists.
Fracture Immobilize the injured area, apply ice to reduce swelling, and seek immediate medical attention for proper diagnosis and treatment.
Heart Attack Call emergency services immediately, have the person sit upright.
Perform CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation) if Necessary: If the person is unresponsive and not breathing normally, you should begin CPR.
To perform CPR, follow these steps:
Place the heel of your hand on the center of the person's chest.
Place your other hand on top of the first hand and interlock your fingers.
Position yourself, so your shoulders are directly above your hands, and push hard and fast, aiming for a rate of 100 to 120 compressions per minute.
Help the Person Sit Upright: If the person is conscious, help them into a comfortable sitting position. This can help ease their breathing and reduce the strain on their heart.
Administer Aspirin if Not Allergic: If the person is conscious and not allergic to aspirin, they can chew a regular strength aspirin (325mg) to help reduce clotting.
High Blood Pressure (Hypertension) Encourage a healthy lifestyle with a balanced diet and regular exercise, and monitor blood pressure regularly. Consult a doctor for proper medication and management.
Electric Shock Turn off the source of electricity if possible, do not touch the person directly, and call for emergency medical assistance immediately.
